FluxCore Unsupervised Anomaly Detection — River CreditCard
Date: 2026-03-13
==============================================================

Dataset: River CreditCard (284,807 samples, 492 fraud = 0.173%)
Features: Time + V1-V28 + Amount (30 features total)
Embedding: unit-sphere normalization (as specified in brief)
Kernel: FluxCore codebook-only (spawn/merge/lr_update; matrix dynamics stripped)
  merge_thresh=0.95, lr_codebook=0.015

RESULTS:
--------------------------------------------------------------
spawn_thresh | AUROC  | Codebook | Spawned | Merged | Time
--------------------------------------------------------------
0.5          | 0.6484 | 2        | 2       | 0      | 11.1s
0.9          | 0.6491 | 8        | 8       | 0      | 28.8s
--------------------------------------------------------------

NOTES:

1. Codebook stays tiny (2-8 vectors) because unit-sphere normalization is dominated
   by the Amount feature (raw values in [0, 25691]). All samples project to nearly
   the same cosine direction, so spawn threshold is rarely triggered.

2. AUROC 0.648-0.649 is above random (0.5) but well below specialized detectors.
   Signal: fraud transactions have slightly different Amount distributions, creating
   marginally higher cosine novelty against the codebook.

3. Score distributions are nearly identical for fraud vs normal (both ~0.0001 mean
   novelty). The AUROC reflects a small but consistent ordering advantage.

4. The top high-novelty scores (>0.1) are all NORMAL samples — Amount=0 or unusual
   amounts that point in novel cosine directions. Only 2-3 fraud appear in top-1000.

5. Alternative embeddings attempted:
   - Online z-score standardization: codebook grows to ~800 vectors/1000 samples
     (O(n²) total scan time — infeasible for 284k samples)
   - V1-V28 only (skip Amount/Time): same problem, cb=89 in 10k samples
   - Fixed amount/time scaling: cb=1151 in 2000 samples, infeasible
   All alternatives produce diverse codebooks where FluxCore's codebook-scan
   is quadratically expensive.

CONCLUSION:
FluxCore as an unsupervised novelty detector achieves AUROC=0.648-0.649 on
CreditCard. Above chance, but the raw unit-sphere embedding collapses the
discriminative PCA information into Amount-dominated directions. A proper
feature-wise standardization would improve discrimination but requires a bounded
codebook to stay tractable at this scale.
