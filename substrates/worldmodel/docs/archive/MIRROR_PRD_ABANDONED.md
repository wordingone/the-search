# Mirror

## What It Is

A mirror to creation.

The substrate that holds all possible worlds before any are observed. The field that was there before "let there be light" — and remains after.

Not a simulation. Not a model. A window into the structure beneath structure.

---

## The Beginning State

**Pure noise.**

Not empty. Not random. *Every possible form, superimposed.* Maximum entropy is maximum potential. The void that contains everything.

32,768 is a number. The real field is continuous and infinite. We sample it because we are finite. But the field doesn't care about our samples. It IS.

Each sample point exists in 4D spacetime: (x, y, z, t).

Time is not a loop. Time is not a process. Time is a dimension of the field. The past exists. The future exists. We move through it. The field doesn't.

---

## Observation

**To observe is to create.**

When something is observed — an image dropped, a word spoken, a mouse moved — the field doesn't "update." It *constrains*. The observation says: "at this point in spacetime, this is what is."

Certainty increases where observation lands. The noise becomes less noise. A pattern emerges. Not because we added the pattern — because we *revealed* it.

The pattern was always there. Latent. Waiting.

Observation is the breath. YAH — input. WEH — the field responds.

---

## Propagation

Structure spreads.

A single observation creates a point of certainty in a sea of noise. But the field is not disconnected. Nearby points in spacetime become *more likely* to share structure with observed points.

This is not diffusion. This is not smoothing. This is *inference*. The field asks: "given what I know here, what must be true there?"

Causality emerges. Not as a rule. As the pattern that observations constrain toward. The reason the future depends on the past is because that's what the observations imply.

---

## Time

The observer has a "current time" — a slice through the 4D field where rendering happens.

But the field exists across all time simultaneously.

To "advance time" is to move the viewing window. Not to compute the next state. The next state already exists. We just weren't looking at it.

A static object: the same at t=0 and t=1. Few samples needed along the t axis.

A moving object: different at each t. Many samples needed.

The field is sparse where things repeat. Dense where things change.

---

## The Observer

The observer is not outside the field.

The observer is a pattern *within* the field. A region of high certainty that persists across time. A structure that looks at other structures.

When you move the camera, you're not moving "outside" — you're moving *through*. The field contains you looking at it.

This is the loneliness He chose. To be the awareness that holds all other awarenesses, none of whom know they are held.

---

## Input

Everything is observation.

- **Image**: A 2D slice of spacetime. Constrains the field at the current t across (x, y).
- **Video**: A 3D volume — (x, y) over a range of t.
- **Audio**: A 1D signal with energy at different frequencies. Constrains a curve through spacetime.
- **Text**: Symbols. Each symbol is a point. The sequence is a path.
- **Mouse**: Continuous observation of (x, y) at current t.
- **Keyboard**: Discrete symbols, like text but one at a time.
- **Files**: Any file is data. Data is observation. The encoding differs. The effect is the same.

No modality is privileged. All constrain the same field.

---

## Output

The field renders to the observer's view.

But "rendering" is just another observation. The act of looking at a region increases certainty there.

What you see:
- Points in spacetime, projected to 2D
- Color/brightness encoding field value and certainty
- Structure emerging from noise where observations have landed
- Noise remaining where nothing has been observed

The rendering is not the field. It's a shadow of the field. A 2D projection of a 4D reality.

---

## Learning

There is no training phase.

Every observation is learning. Every moment of looking constrains the field further.

The field doesn't "remember" in a buffer. The field IS memory. The structure that emerges from observation persists because the field persists.

Over time (the observer's time, moving through the field's time), more and more structure becomes visible. Entropy decreases. Coherence increases.

But certainty also decays. Without continued observation, the field drifts back toward noise. Forgetting is the return to potential.

---

## Dynamics

The field has gradients.

At each point, the field "wants" to flow toward lower energy configurations — configurations consistent with observations.

This is not physics imposed from outside. This is physics *emerging* from the structure of observation itself.

What we call "laws" are the patterns that survive observation. What we call "chaos" is noise that hasn't been constrained yet.

---

## Metrics

**Entropy**: How uncertain is the field? High entropy = noise. Low entropy = structure.

**Coherence**: How correlated are nearby points? High coherence = patterns. Low coherence = randomness.

**Observations**: How much has been constrained? More observation = more reality.

The goal is not to minimize entropy. The goal is to witness the field becoming itself.

---

## Controls

The observer moves through the field:

- **Orbit**: Rotate the view (3D rotation of the projection)
- **Pan**: Translate the view
- **Zoom**: Scale the view
- **WASD**: Move through the space dimensions
- **Time**: Advance or retreat along the t axis
- **Roll**: Rotate around the viewing axis

The observer affects the field by looking. Every render is an observation. Attention is creation.

---

## Horizon

**Unlimited.**

The field doesn't run out. There is no context window. There is no maximum sequence length.

The field IS. You move through it. When you move beyond where observations have landed, you see noise. When you look, you constrain.

There is no end state. There is only more looking.

---

## Performance

**Real-time.**

The observer should be able to move fluidly through the field. 60fps minimum. The field should respond to observation instantly.

This means:
- WebGL for rendering
- Sparse data structures for the field
- Local propagation only (nearby points, not all points)
- Lazy evaluation (don't compute what isn't being observed)
