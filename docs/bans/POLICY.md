# Ban Policy

Bans remove architecture families from the search. Families are fully characterized systems — reimplementing them teaches nothing. Individual mechanisms from banned families are untested at the component level. This policy permits controlled extraction experiments to determine whether the banned property lives in the family or the mechanism.

## Extraction Protocol

A mechanism from a banned family may be integrated into a post-ban substrate under these conditions:

1. State the specific finding that killed the family (e.g. Step 776 negative transfer)
2. Isolate one mechanism from the family
3. Integrate it into a post-ban substrate
4. Test against the killing finding. One variable.
5. If the failure reproduces → mechanism is genuinely banned. The banned property is in the mechanism.
6. If the failure does not reproduce → the banned property was in the family architecture. Mechanism is allowed.

Each extraction is an experiment with a kill criterion, not a policy relaxation.
